Data

1. Characteristics of the Sample: This section explains the respondents of who answered the survey.

1.1 Total Sample Size: A total of 60 respondents successfully completed the survey and provided usable responses.

1.2 Gender

- I chose a pie chart because it shows the percentage of people in each gender category and makes the data easy to understand.

- The gender distribution of respondents was balanced. The pie chart was used because it clearly shows the proportion of respondents in each category and it makes the results easy to understand.

- The gender data shows that the survey responses were almost evenly split between males and females, with both groups making up 48.3% of respondents. This means that the survey results are unlikely to be influenced by one of the gender, making the findings more balanced. Only a small amount of people selected "Other" or preferred not to disclose their gender.

1.3 Age Distribution

- I chose bar graphs because for me, I think bar graphs are the easiest to look at and easy to compare the frequencies of the different gender categories and clearly shows which category has the highest and lowest number of responses.

- The bar graph was used because it allows for easy comparison of the frequencies across different age groups and clearly shows which groups had the highest and lowest numbers of respondents.

- The age distribution shows that respondents came from a wide range of age groups, most of the people are aged 18–24 and 35–44. This suggests that the survey reflects the opinions of both younger adults and middle-aged adults. Since the only a few people are over 65 years old, the results may not fully represent the views of older adults.
